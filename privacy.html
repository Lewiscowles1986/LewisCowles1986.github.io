Privacy Policy

Coverage

This covers https://www.lewiscowles.co.uk website and software created linking to this policy

Browser plugins

Lewis authors browser plugins for popular web-browsers.

None of these collect your personal information to send to Lewis or anyone he is aware of.

These plugins are usually to give you control over the web you interact with including saving logs of bookmarks in markdown, or changing webpages to remove trigger words or offensive terms.

Lewis does work on plugins that work with private businesses and those are not covered under this privacy policy.

Lewis does include a link to his website in some of his work.

Personally identifiable information

Lewis Cowles does not collect PII. At the time of writing, Lewis is not a data controller and has no plans to become one.

While Lewis Cowles can and will support customers collecting, using and providing goods and services that use such information, he will ensure that the highest standards are maintained and that he ask the right questions to minimize unnecessary data capture.

Cookies

If you visit our login page, he will set a temporary cookie to determine if your browser accepts cookies. This cookie contains no personal data and is discarded when you close your browser.

When you log in, he will also set up several cookies to save your login information and your screen display choices. Login cookies last for two days, and screen options cookies last for a year. If you select “Remember Me”, your login will persist for two heeks. If you log out of your account, the login cookies will be removed.

If you edit or publish an article, an additional cookie will be saved in your browser. This cookie includes no personal data and simply indicates the post ID of the article you just edited. It expires after 1 day.

If you use other connected systems with the user details including password, login session will be maintained using a cookie. If you are redirected to a third-party service, via iframe or otherwise, those resources are not covered under this privacy policy, but will be covered under that domain policy.

Server logs

If your browser is transmitting PII automatically, this may show up in our server logs.

Your data is encrypted using TLS if you access this website via HTTPS (padlock in address bar), and he do not make use of the data outside of remitting errors, and basic checks.

Monitoring

Sections of our website, back-end and applications may monitor feature usage. This is attributed to a user account and will never be deleted.

Upon the closure of a customer account this data is held as a record of what was. he reserve the right to remove data from our systems, but such data is about business, and not you as a person or your private life.

Sharing data

Lewis Cowles does not share your private data with anyone. he does not sell it, and he does not give it away.

Lewis Cowles offers an SSL certificate when accessing his website.

If you approach us about a project, limited information that is not personally identifiable may be used to work with third-parties to ensure service delivery goals can be, and are met.

For users that register on our website (if any), he also store the personal information they provide in their user profile.

All users can see, edit, or delete their personal information at any time (except they cannot change their username). website administrators can also see and edit that information.

How he protects your data

Lewis Cowles believes the best cure is prevention. For this reason Lewis Cowles does not collect or deal in private data.

Where he deal with your private data, he will work to ensure PII is encrypted at rest and in transport; that he work from machines with virus protection and updates, most of which is Open Source or provided by large industry vendors.

Lewis Cowles is not a data controller so there really should be no likelihood of this, and he has no prior incidents.
